The Butler-Williams Scholars Program is accepting applications for its 2023 program, which provides opportunities for junior faculty and researchers new to the field of aging to gain insight about research on aging from various perspectives. The program includes lectures, seminars, and small group discussions in research design relative to aging, including issues relevant to aging of ethnic and racial minorities. The program also offers consultation on the development of research interests and advice on preparing and submitting research grant applications to NIA. Applicants must hold a doctoral degree, be a U.S. citizen, non-citizen national, or permanent resident, and actively considering research in the field of aging. Researchers with an interest in health disparities research are encouraged to apply. The program is accepting applications until April 21, 2023, and offers opportunities for professional development.
