Alzheimer's disease is the most common cause of dementia, affecting people's memory, recognition, and behavior. It can affect people of all ages but is more common in those over 65. There are around 900,000 people in the UK with dementia, and families can find it challenging to deal with. Support is available from special groups, charities, and people. The article encourages seeking help from the Alzheimer's Society or Childline and includes news stories and links to CBBC content.
